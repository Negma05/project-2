Food Haven Menu

Appetizers

  • Brik à l'œuf

    A traditional Tunisian dish made from thin pastry filled with egg, tuna, capers, parsley, and sometimes harissa. The pastry is folded into a triangle and fried until crispy. It's light, crunchy, and packed with flavor, making it an excellent start to any meal.

  • Chorba Frik

    A popular soup in Tunisia made from lamb, bulgur wheat, tomatoes, and a mix of aromatic spices like cumin and coriander. It's hearty, warm, and comforting, often served during Ramadan but enjoyed year-round as a flavorful appetizer.

  • Salade Méchouia

    A grilled vegetable salad made from peppers, tomatoes, onions, and garlic, seasoned with olive oil, lemon juice, and sometimes tuna or eggs. The smokiness of the grilled vegetables combined with the tangy dressing creates a refreshing and light appetizer.

Main Courses

  • Couscous with Lamb and Vegetables

    A staple in Tunisian cuisine, this dish features steamed semolina grains topped with a stew of lamb, carrots, zucchini, chickpeas, and a blend of spices. The rich and savory flavors of the lamb are complemented by the sweetness of the vegetables, making it a satisfying and complete meal.

  • Tajine Maadnous

    A Tunisian baked omelet made with chicken, parsley, cheese, and eggs, seasoned with turmeric and cinnamon. It's a versatile dish that can be served hot or cold, with a texture similar to a frittata. It's rich, filling, and perfect for a comforting main course.

  • Ojja with Merguez

    A spicy tomato-based stew cooked with eggs and Merguez sausage, seasoned with garlic, cumin, and harissa. It's a bold and flavorful dish that's quick to prepare, making it a popular choice for a hearty and satisfying main course.

Desserts

  • Baklawa

    A sweet pastry made of layers of filo dough filled with chopped nuts, such as almonds or pistachios, and sweetened with honey or syrup. The layers are baked until golden and crispy, creating a rich and indulgent dessert that's a favorite in Tunisian cuisine.

  • Makroud

    A semolina-based pastry filled with dates or figs and flavored with orange blossom water. The pastries are fried and then soaked in honey, giving them a sweet and fragrant taste. They are chewy on the inside and crispy on the outside, making them a delightful way to end a meal.

  • Bambalouni

    Tunisian-style doughnuts made from deep-fried dough, usually sprinkled with sugar or soaked in honey. These light and fluffy doughnuts are crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, offering a simple yet satisfying dessert option.
